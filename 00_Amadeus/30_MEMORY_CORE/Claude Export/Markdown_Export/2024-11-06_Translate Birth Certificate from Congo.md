RCG: 10308

Reason for which she seized the Céans Tribunal to
obtain a judgment allowing her to obtain
a birth certificate in her favor;

Having been given the floor for its opinion, the Public Ministry
requested the Tribunal to grant her petition;

In law, it follows from Article 116, paragraph 1 of the
family code as modified and completed to date that
"any birth occurring on the territory of the Democratic
Republic of Congo must be declared to the civil status officer
of the father's or mother's residence within
ninety days following the birth";

Article 106, paragraph 1 of the same code provides
that "the lack of civil status document can be remedied by a
judgment rendered by the Peace Tribunal or by the tribunal for
children, as the case may be, upon simple petition presented to the tribunal
of the place where the document should have been drawn up";

Ruling on this matter, the Tribunal notes, in
view of the petition and the elements in the file, that the
petitioner is the person best placed to seize the
Céans Tribunal and that she demonstrates an obvious interest in having
her birth registered;

Furthermore, it appears from the aforementioned documents that the
petitioner was born in Lubumbashi on 24/11/1960 from the marital
union of Mr. NKUNDA KATAMBA Tuedor and
Mrs. NYE MABU Rebecca and that at the time of her
birth, her aforementioned parents resided at the address
indicated above;

Consequently, the Tribunal will declare admissible and
well-founded the action brought by the petitioner and will declare it
founded;

Therefore, will order the civil status officer of
the Municipality of Lemba to register her birth
in its registers for the current year and issue the related
certificate;

The Tribunal will charge the costs to her;

FOR THESE REASONS;

The Tribunal;

Ruling publicly and adversarially with
