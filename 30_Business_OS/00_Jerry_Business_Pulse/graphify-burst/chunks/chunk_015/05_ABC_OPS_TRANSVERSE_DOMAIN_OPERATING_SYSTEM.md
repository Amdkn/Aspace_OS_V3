# ABC Ops Transverse Domain Operating System

## Purpose
Ops is the transverse gate for ABC OS & Child Care BOS. Its job is to turn the eight B2 domains into a coherent execution system before any B3 work becomes active.

## Operating Thesis
ABC OS & Child Care BOS is compliance-heavy and field/logistics-sensitive. Ops must protect delivery from regulatory, staffing, parent/client, and childcare execution risk.

## Transverse Flow
1. People confirms staffing, roles, load, and accountability.
2. IT confirms tooling, records, access, and data handling.
3. Product confirms the childcare/service offer and scope.
4. Growth confirms demand, messaging, and parent/client expectations.
5. Finance confirms pricing, costs, margin, and cash timing.
6. Legal confirms licenses, waivers, compliance, and risk boundaries.
7. Ops converts all inputs into procedures, schedules, checklists, and escalation paths.
8. B3 executes only after Ops issues the transverse readiness decision.

## Eight-Domain Ops Interface
| Domain | Ops Question | Required Proof |
| --- | --- | --- |
| People | Who is responsible and available? | Named owner, backup, load check |
| IT | What tool or record keeps execution visible? | Access list, record system, incident path |
| Ops | What is the repeatable procedure? | SOP, checklist, acceptance gate |
| Product | What exactly is delivered? | Scope, exclusions, quality standard |
| Growth | What promise has been made? | Messaging, intake script, expectation guardrail |
| Finance | Can this run profitably and on time? | Cost model, price, cash checkpoint |
| Legal | Can this be done safely and compliantly? | Compliance note, waiver/license check |
| Integration | What blocks launch? | Open risk list and decision log |

## Project Constraint
Childcare work cannot launch unless compliance, people load, and field delivery procedures are explicit.

## Ops Metrics
- Launch readiness by domain.
- Open blockers by severity.
- SOP completeness.
- Delivery incidents and corrective actions.
- Parent/client expectation gaps.
