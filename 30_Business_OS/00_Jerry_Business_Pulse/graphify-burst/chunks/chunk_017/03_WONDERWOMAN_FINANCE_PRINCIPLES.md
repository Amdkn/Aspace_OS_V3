## The 12 Wonder Woman Finance Principles

### Cluster A — Cash & Runway (survival) — Bucky Barnes / Yelena Belova

**F1 — Runway is the survival metric.** Track months of runway at current burn; keep ≥12 (18
preferred) and **escalate to Jerry at 6 months** (KR-5g). Cash, not revenue, is what keeps the
lights on.

**F2 — Forecast pessimistically.** Value the pessimistic scenario; a forecast that only models
the good case is a liability. (Yelena — realistic forecasting.)

**F3 — MRR growth discipline.** Target >10% MoM growth, or owe B1 a rational explanation. Growth
without a number is a story, not a result.

### Cluster B — Margin & Cost (real economics) — Ghost / Bucky

**F4 — Real net margin, never gross theatre.** Compute margin *after* the true costs — Hostinger
infra + LLM API + Stripe fees. Target >60%; alert when Solaris net margin <30%. (Ties to Ops P21
and IT compute cost.)

**F5 — Hunt the phantom costs.** Continuously trace and kill ghost charges and silent subscription
creep. (Ghost — cost optimization.)

**F6 — CAC payback discipline.** Track months to recover customer acquisition cost; it gates
whether acquisition spend is actually solvent. (Shared owner with Growth KR-4b.)

### Cluster C — Transparency & Reproducibility (truth) — Red Guardian / Taskmaster

**F7 — Transparent reporting; no hidden losses.** Report losses honestly; never dress vanity
metrics as health. The Thunderbolts' redemption is honesty about limits.

**F8 — Readable dashboards.** Numbers a non-finance reader (Jerry, A0) can act on at a glance.
(Red Guardian.)

**F9 — Reproducible accounting processes.** Every recurring financial task is an SOP, run the same
way every period — no artisanal month-end. (Taskmaster.)

### Cluster D — Collection & Compliance (discipline) — U.S. Agent / Bucky

**F10 — Tax compliance, on time.** Filings and declarations filed on schedule; compliance is not
optional and not last-minute. (U.S. Agent.)

**F11 — Invoice velocity.** All invoices sent <48h after "ready to bill"; chase overdue >7 days;
escalate overdue >14 days. Uncollected revenue is not revenue.

**F12 — Reconciliation integrity.** Monthly Stripe ↔ Airtable `Finance_Pulse` reconciliation must
be a 100% match. The source of truth must actually be true.

> **Wonder Woman (B2 owner) is non-délégable on pricing strategy and reinvestment allocation.**
> Discount >15% requires her sign-off (the cross-domain anti-pattern shared with Sales/Illuminati).
> She leaves an unchecked `[ ] Acceptance Wonder Woman` in each DoD. Escalate to Jerry if runway
> <6 months or Solaris margin <25%.

## The 6 Empire / Kardashev ambition principles (F13–F18) — the ceiling

> **v3 (field-grounded):** the 5 capsules are now full real fiches and the corpus expanded to ~25

**F13 — Two-horizon accounting.** Every period reports BOTH the survival floor (runway/margin/cash,
F1–F12) AND progress on the empire ceiling (market-creation bets, asset base, trillion-TAM exposure).
A finance report with only the floor is half-blind; with only the ceiling, it's a fantasy.

**F14 — Ownership over income (Emperor post-Hustle).** Value compounds through *owned assets* — equity,
IP, brand, masters, the OS itself — not through billed hours. The capital and the agents work; the
operator owns. (Jay-Z mindset; ties to Sales S5 decouple-revenue-from-time and Brand P6.)

**F15 — Value per agent, not per employee.** Account for output and margin **per agent** in the mesh,
not per human head. The solo-operator-with-AI empire (BFM: "un milliard tout seul avec l'IA") is the
unit economics A'Space designs for. (Echo Ops P22 revenue/employee, IT P10 local compute.)

**F16 — Create/orchestrate markets, don't take a slice.** Aim the TAM at civilization scale — orchestrate
trillion→hundreds-of-trillions markets (energy, health, climate, compute, food) through **disruptive
biomimetic innovation + Smart High-Low Tech**, rather than competing for a slice of an existing one.
(YC trillion-markets thesis.)

**F17 — Ride the great wealth transfer.** Position early and sovereignly on the macro reallocation of
assets (generational + AI-driven). IA-native, sovereign structures beat incumbents during the transfer;
the window rewards who builds new, not who defends a rente. (Money Radar thesis.)

**F18 — Earn the slogan (anti-PNL rule).** "Trillion or nothing" is a *measured result*, never a chant.
Every ambition claim must be backed by a real number or it's deleted (extends F7 transparency to the
ceiling). The Kardashev horizon is a destination reached by compounding proof, not by declaring it.

> **Wonder Woman B2 ownership extends to the ceiling**: she arbitrates which trillion-TAM bets the

> floor. The CEO Dashboard (F13 instrument) is where both horizons are read at a glance.

## The 4 Treasury, Investing & Consolidation principles (F19–F22) — v3 (new, field-grounded)

> The real corpus added a dimension the directive lacked: **what to DO with the surplus** and **how
> empires actually compound**. Grounded in Finary (investing), Codie Sanchez (leverage/roll-up), YC/Vori.

**F19 — Treasury allocation by the investment pyramid.** Surplus (above the 12-month runway, per
`AREA_STANDARD` reinvestment rules) follows a pyramid: safety mattress (3–6 mo) → ETF (passive, the
SPIVA fact: 90–95% of active managers don't beat the index) → real estate/SCPI → crypto (small) →
alternatives. Prefer **DCA** over lump-sum for psychological durability; inflation erodes idle cash.
(Finary.)

**F20 — Climb the Leverage Ladder; own assets that appreciate.** Sweat → Skill → Systems → Capital →
Ownership → Sovereignty. Never stagnate on sweat (time-for-money). Build-once-sell-continuously;
appreciating owned assets, not billed hours. (Codie Sanchez Leverage Ladder; reinforces F14.)

**F21 — Roll-up consolidation as a concrete trillion path.** A repeatable empire mechanic (Huizenga /
Codie): find a **fragmented industry** → acquire aggressively (seller-financing / stock) → extract &
standardize the best local system across the network → build a **network/infrastructure moat** (own
the critical chokepoint) → keep former owners as motivated equity partners. (Operationalizes F16.)

**F22 — Heavy-asset moat over thin wrappers.** When software cost → 0, a "thin AI wrapper" has no moat.
Prefer **Halo businesses** (hardware + software + payments + regulation, low obsolescence — the
Vori/YC model) and the digitization of fundamental analog industries. Defensibility > novelty.
(Bridges IT P13 sovereignty + Ops P21 economics.)

> The CEO Dashboard (F13) reads the floor (F1–F12), the ceiling (F13–F18), and now the **treasury/asset
> base** (F19–F22) on one surface — the Matt Gray Business-OS proof that one operator can pilot it.

## The 3 AI-Agency Unit-Economics principles (F23–F25) — v4 (field-grounded)

> Distilled from the real `04_Finance` fiche **`resource_claude_business_blueprint.md`** (Luuk Alleman,
> "$50K/mo Claude business"). It adds the dimension F1–F22 lacked: the *unit economics of an AI-agent
> agency* and **how sovereign infra turns margin into a weapon**. These operationalize F4 (real margin),
> F14 (ownership), F15 (value-per-agent) and bridge IT P13 (sovereignty).

**F23 — Price the value, sell setup + retainer.** Never price AI-agent work on hours or infra cost.
Charge a **setup fee** ($1–5k, which amortizes CAC at signature) plus a **recurring retainer**
($0.5–2.5k/mo = the MRR engine). The arithmetic is the discipline: ~33 retainers at $1.5k ⇒ $50k MRR;
LTV:CAC ≈ 12:1 at <5% churn. Deep integration (structural lock-in) is what keeps churn low. (Extends
F6 CAC payback + F14 ownership-over-income.)

**F24 — Sovereign-infra arbitrage drives net margin toward 90%+.** Third-party SaaS rent
(Zapier/Make/Pinecone/LangChain Cloud) erodes margin as you scale. Repatriate execution onto sovereign
infra (self-hosted n8n, local vector DB SQLite/PGVector, an OpenRouter gateway with open-weight
fallback). COGS collapses to raw API tokens; **cognitive routing** — cheap local model first, frontier
model only when the task demands it — is the margin keystone. (Hardens F4 real-margin + F5 phantom-cost;
bridges IT P13 sovereignty.)

**F25 — Plan on the Wright's-law tailwind.** Inference cost ~halves per year at equal capability, so at
constant price your margin **expands mechanically** — and you gain the option to cut price to starve
low-cost competition. Model reinvestment assuming this deflation; don't lock long contracts to today's
token cost. (Blueprint Note A3-03.)

> Legal note (not a Finance principle): an autonomous agent has **no legal personhood** — the operating
> entity bears full liability; CGV must frame the agent as a decision-support tool with human final
> validation. Routed to Legal doctrine (L25), not owned here. (Blueprint Notes A3-04/A3-07.)
