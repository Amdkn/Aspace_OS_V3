# AaaS Ops Transverse Domain Operating System

## Purpose

Ops is the delivery spine of `00 Agency as a Service`. Its job is not only to write SOPs; it converts every domain promise into a repeatable operating path.

If AaaS cannot be delivered repeatedly, it is not Business Done, even if Growth creates demand, Sales closes deals, or Product demos well.

## Operating Thesis

AaaS sells outcomes. Ops protects those outcomes from becoming bespoke chaos.

Every offer must pass through this sequence:

```text
Growth signal
  -> Sales qualification
  -> Product scope
  -> Ops delivery design
  -> IT runtime/access boundary
  -> Finance price/margin check
  -> Legal claims/data/IP review
  -> People owner/load map
  -> Ops launch readiness
  -> Delivery proof
  -> Renewal/expansion learning
```

## The 8-Domain Ops Interface

| Domain | What Ops Requires | What Ops Returns |
|---|---|---|
| Growth | ICP, pain, channel, expected volume, promise language. | Delivery capacity constraint, proof assets, case-study capture path. |
| Sales | Qualified prospect, scope expectation, objections, promised outcome. | Delivery feasibility, handoff checklist, red/yellow/green promise rating. |
| Product | MVP scope, workflow, exclusions, demo path. | Supportability rating, delivery SOP, QA checklist. |
| IT | Runtime path, access model, backup/recovery, data boundary. | Operational runbook requirements and incident triggers. |
| Finance | Price floor, margin target, cost model, payment cadence. | Delivery cost assumptions and hidden labor risks. |
| Legal | Claims boundary, data/IP terms, contract constraints. | Delivery wording constraints and compliance checklist. |
| People | Owner map, operators, reviewer, training needs, load risk. | Role-specific SOPs, escalation paths, weekly review cadence. |
| Ops | Intake, delivery, QA, exception handling, improvement loop. | PASS / CONDITIONAL / BLOCKED launch gate. |

## AaaS Delivery Phases

| Phase | Name | Exit Proof |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Offer intake | Sales handoff packet accepted by Ops. |
| 1 | Scope lock | Product scope + exclusions approved. |
| 2 | Delivery design | SOP, QA checklist, timeline, and owner map drafted. |
| 3 | Runtime check | IT access/runtime/data boundaries confirmed. |
| 4 | Margin/trust check | Finance and Legal constraints accepted. |
| 5 | Crew/agent readiness | People confirms owners, load, and training. |
| 6 | Delivery execution | Artifact delivered with QA evidence. |
| 7 | Learning loop | Lead/Lag captured; SOP updated or blocker logged. |

## Launch Readiness Levels

| Status | Meaning | Action |
|---|---|---|
| PASS | Ops can repeat delivery with known owners and constraints. | Launch or continue. |
| CONDITIONAL | Delivery possible but a named risk remains. | Launch only with owner, risk, and review date. |
| BLOCKED | Delivery promise cannot be honored repeatably. | Stop launch; return to B2 Council. |

## Ops Metrics

| Metric | Type | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Handoff completeness | Lead | 100% of Sales->Ops packets complete before delivery. |
| QA pass rate | Lead | >=90% first-pass delivery checklist completion. |
| Rework rate | Lag | <10% of deliverables require major redo. |
| Owner dependency | Lag | No recurring task has A0/Jerry as required operator. |
| Exception time-to-route | Lead | Blocker routed within 24h. |

## Anti-Patterns

- Sales promises delivery before Ops feasibility.
- Product ships a prototype with no support path.
- IT hides runtime assumptions in one operator's head.
- Finance prices without labor cost.
- Legal reviews after public claims are already published.
- People assigns ownership after delivery breaks.
