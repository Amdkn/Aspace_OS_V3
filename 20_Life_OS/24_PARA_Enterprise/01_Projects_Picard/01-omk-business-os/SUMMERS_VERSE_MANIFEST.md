## ICP Variants — OMK Business OS Modes

### Solaris (Visual-First)
**Trigger**: OMK client primary need is brand/visual identity systems
**Profile**: Clients who buy on aesthetics, storytelling, brand impression. Decision driven by portfolio/samples.
**Mode markers**: High CAC, high ASP, brand-conscious, speed-first buyers.
**Primary books**: `$100M Offers` (Hormozi), `Building a StoryBrand` (Miller)

### Nexus (Data-First / Expert Knowledge)
**Trigger**: OMK client primary need is business systems, processes, and operational expertise
**Profile**: Clients who buy on credentials, case studies, thought leadership. Decision driven by evidence and systematization.
**Mode markers**: Longer sales cycle, higher ASP, eval-intensive buyers.
**Primary books**: `Expert Secrets` (Canter), `The Consulting Bible` (Maister), `The E-Myth Revisited` (Gerber)

### Orbiter (Field-First / Logistics)
**Trigger**: >$500K MRR, execution/logistics-heavy client engagements
**Profile**: Clients who buy on capacity, reliability, delivery guarantees. Decision driven by track record.
**Mode markers**: Highest ASP, relationship-driven, contract-heavy.
**Primary books**: `Built to Sell` (Warrilow), `The E-Myth Revisited` (Gerber)

**OMK Primary Mode: Nexus** — OMK Business OS is fundamentally a systems/expertise engagement. Clients hire OMK to build a complete business OS — not for visuals, but for operational infrastructure. Nexus mode is the default. Solaris is activated if brand systems become the primary deliverable. Orbiter is activated if OMK scales to franchise-level execution.
