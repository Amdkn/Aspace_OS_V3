# RILCOT Ops Transverse Domain Operating System

## Purpose
Ops is the transverse gate for RILCOT Members Space OS. Its job is to convert the eight B2 domains into repeatable member-space operations.

## Operating Thesis
RILCOT Members Space OS depends on recurring member activation and knowledge operations. Ops must make onboarding, community cadence, feedback, and knowledge capture repeatable.

## Transverse Flow
1. People confirms member roles, moderation roles, and operating ownership.
2. IT confirms member-space tools, access, data, and support paths.
3. Product confirms the member experience and service promise.
4. Growth confirms acquisition, activation, and retention motion.
5. Finance confirms membership economics and recurring cost.
6. Legal confirms terms, privacy, and community boundaries.
7. Ops converts the whole into rituals, runbooks, checklists, and escalation.
8. B3 executes only after Ops issues the transverse readiness decision.

## Eight-Domain Ops Interface
| Domain | Ops Question | Required Proof |
| --- | --- | --- |
| People | Who hosts, moderates, and follows up? | Role map and cadence owner |
| IT | Where does the member system live? | Tool map, access, support path |
| Ops | What rituals run every week/month? | Runbook and calendar |
| Product | What member value is delivered? | Experience map and quality bar |
| Growth | How are members activated and retained? | Funnel and retention loop |
| Finance | Is the model sustainable? | Membership economics |
| Legal | What are the community boundaries? | Terms/privacy/rules checkpoint |
| Integration | What breaks the member experience? | Blocker and risk log |

## Project Constraint
Community value decays if rituals, ownership, and feedback loops are not operationalized.

## Ops Metrics
- Member onboarding completion.
- Ritual cadence reliability.
- Feedback loop closure.
- Support response time.
- Knowledge capture rate.
