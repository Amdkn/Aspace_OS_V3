## 2. Capital Architecture

*Framework refs: Psychology of Money (Housel), Rich Dad Poor Dad (Kiyosaki), Millionaire Fastlane (DeMarco)*

### Asset vs Liability Mindset

```
ASSET: Puts money in your pocket monthly (dividends, royalties, equity growth, cashflow)
LIABILITY: Takes money from your pocket monthly (debt service, maintenance drain, dependence)
```

**Rule J02-001**: Every revenue decision must classify the outcome as asset or liability.
- Revenue that creates dependency without equity build = LIABILITY
- Revenue that builds equity without ongoing extraction = ASSET
- Revenue that extracts value while building nothing = REJECT

### Wealth Architecture Tiers

| Tier | Definition | Reinvestment Rule |
|------|------------|-------------------|
| T1 Safety | 6 months runway in cash | Never reinvest. Protect. |
| T2 Independence | 80% coverage ratio, 12 months runway | Reinvest max 20% of surplus |
| T3 Acceleration | 100%+ coverage, 18+ months runway | Reinvest up to 50% of surplus |
| T4 Sovereignty | Passive income > 3x burn, no debt | Full discretion. A0 decides. |

**Rule J02-002**: No business expansion may reduce current tier without A0 explicit sign-off.

### Wealth Velocity Gates

- **Lane 1 (Slowlane)**: Active income reinvested into slow assets. Acceptable for T1 safety.
- **Lane 2 (Fastlane)**: Business equity builds that compound. Required for T2/T3.
- **Lane 3 (Sovereignty)**: Passive income engines that run without owner time. Target for T4.

**Rule J02-003**: Every Summer's Verse proposal must identify which lane it operates in.

## 3. Independence Threshold

*Framework refs: Your Money or Your Life (Robin), Simple Path to Wealth (Collins)*

### The Number That Gates Expansion

**Independence Number = Monthly Burn × 12**

When passive income reaches this number, LD02 is satisfied and LD06 unlocks fully.

### Independence Threshold Rules

**Rule J03-001 — The 4% Rule Baseline**:
```
Target Passive Income = Total Invested Assets × 0.04
```
This assumes a 4% withdrawal rate. Conservative during accumulation phase. Do not count home equity.

**Rule J03-002 — The Coverage Ratio**:
```
Coverage Ratio = Monthly Passive Income / Monthly Essential Burn
```
Essential burn = A0's non-negotiable personal + business floor costs. Excludes growth investments.

| Coverage Ratio | Status | Expansion Permission |
|----------------|--------|---------------------|
| < 60% | RED | NO expansion. Defend only. |
| 60–79% | ORANGE | Minimal. Only if increases coverage within 90 days. |
| 80–99% | YELLOW | Cautious. Max 20% surplus reinvest. |
| 100%+ | GREEN | Normal expansion permitted. |

**Rule J03-003 — The Runway Floor**:
```
Runway = Cash on Hand / Monthly Net Burn
```
Do not count committed future revenue until received.

| Runway | Status | Action |
|--------|--------|--------|
| < 3 months | RED | Full stop. No new commitments. Preserve cash. |
| 3–5 months | ORANGE | Survive mode. Revenue focus only. |
| 6–11 months | YELLOW | Cautious growth. One commitment at a time. |
| 12+ months | GREEN | Normal operations. |

### What "Independence" Actually Costs

Track these as the true cost of sovereignty:
- Housing (not mortgage > 28% gross income)
- Healthcare (marketplace + deductible reserve)
- Basic mobility (vehicle + insurance reserve)
- Food sovereignty (home + quality protein)
- A0 presence protection (no-work zones, family time reserves)

## 4. Automation Banking

*Framework ref: I Will Teach You to Be Rich (Robin)*

### Automated Banking Architecture

**Rule J04-001 — The 6-Account Structure**:

| Account | Purpose | Trigger |
|---------|---------|---------|
| BUFFER | 1 month essential burn | Automatic. Refills from income. |
| REINVEST | Business growth allocation | % of income. Never goes to personal. |
| INDEPENDENCE | Long-term compound | 20% of surplus after buffer. |
| PROTECT | Insurance deductible reserve | $X monthly until funded. |
| PLAY | A0 presence fund | 5% of income. Non-negotiable. |
| GIVE | Charitable/gift reserve | 1% of income. Builds optionality. |

**Rule J04-002 — Automation Mandate**:
All accounts fund automatically on income receipt. A0 sets and forgets. No manual allocation during the month.

**Rule J04-003 — Monthly Review**:
Jerry Nexus reviews automation performance on day 1 of each month. Exceptions:
- Buffer fell below 1 month → refill immediately, no expansion
- Independence account fell → skip expansion quarter
- PLAY account was raided → A0 debrief required

### Systems That Run Without A0 Attention

1. **Billing/collections**: Automated invoicing. Payment reminders automated.
2. **Tax reserve**: 25% of revenue auto-streams to tax reserve quarterly.
3. **Payroll**: If applicable, runs on set schedule.
4. **Subscriptions**: Annual renewals only. No monthly subscriptions without 90-day trial.

## 5. Presence Architecture

*Framework refs: 4-Hour Workweek (Ferriss), War of Art (Pressfield), Can't Hurt Me (Goggins)*

### Protecting Presence from Business Extraction

**Rule J05-001 — The Presence Floor**:
```
Family Presence Score = 10 - (Work Hours / Ideal Hours) × 10
```
Ideal hours = hours A0 would work in an unconstrained world. Baseline: 6 hours/day max for presence.

| Score | Status | Business Expansion |
|-------|--------|-------------------|
| > 8/10 | GREEN | Normal expansion |
| 6–8/10 | YELLOW | Monitor weekly. 10% max increase. |
| 4–6/10 | ORANGE | RED threshold warning. Freeze. |
| < 4/10 | RED | Full stop. Revenue on hold until score recovers. |

**Rule J05-002 — The War of Art Boundary**:
```
RESISTANCE = Any activity that substitutes for doing the real work.
```
Business activities that produce immediate dopamine but no compound value = RESISTANCE.
When RESISTANCE is high (score > 7/10), Jerry Nexus blocks new projects and forces consolidation.

**Rule J05-003 — The Goggins Accountability**:
```
Monthly Hard Thing Count = 3 non-negotiable completions
```
These are not business tasks. These are physical/mental disciplines that maintain identity integrity:
- One physical challenge (run, fast, cold exposure)
- One learning challenge (completed course chapter, language session)
- One creative challenge (writing, music, building)

If A0 misses 2 consecutive months of Hard Things, Family Presence is compromised — Revenue expansion pauses.

**Rule J05-004 — The Ferriss DEAL Method**:
For any new business commitment, apply DEAL:

- **D** iscard: Can this be declined, delegated, or deferred?
- **E** liminate: Can this be eliminated entirely?
- **A** utomate: Can this run without A0 after setup?
- **L** iberalize: Can this be done 80% as well with half the time?

If answer is NO to all four and the commitment exceeds 10 hrs/month, A0 explicit approval required.

## 6. Identity & Sovereignty

*Framework refs: Naval Almanack (Jorgenson), Sovereign Individual (Davidson/Rees-Mogg), 12 Rules (Peterson)*

### Philosophical Spine for Decisions

**Naval Rule — The 4 Outputs**:
```
All time and energy must produce one of:
1. Money (compounding knowledge + equity)
2. Health (physical + mental)
3. Relationships (deep, not wide)
4. Freedom (no dependent commitments)
```
If a business activity does not advance at least one of these four, reject it.

**Sovereign Individual Rule — The 3 Freedoms**:
```
Freedom 1: Time (when you work)
Freedom 2: Location (where you work)
Freedom 3: Structure (how you work)
```
Any expansion that reduces any of the three freedoms requires A0 explicit approval.

**Peterson Rule — The 12 Minimums for Jerry Nexus Area**:

1. **Tell the truth, or immediately correct course.** — No revenue lie. If projections were wrong, update immediately.
2. **Do not do things you hate.** — Revenue from extraction of values = hate. Reject.
3. **Work as hard as you can.** — On the RIGHT things. Not on resistance activities.
4. **Build something of value.** — Equity, not just income. Lasts after the work stops.
5. **Be precise in speech.** — No "we'll figure it out" commitments. Numbers only.
6. **Do not avoid necessary difficulty.** — Confront the hard revenue conversations. Don't defer.
7. **Make at least one person genuinely better off.** — Per transaction. Per client. Per quarter.
8. **Reduce uncertainty before increasing leverage.** — Don't bet the runway on unproven.
9. **Don't skip what you most need to do.** — Presence first. Business second.
10. **Treat yourself like someone you are responsible for helping.** — Protect your time like it has value. Because it does.
11. **Do no harm to those closest to you.** — Revenue that damages family = prohibited.
12. **Make meaning, not money.** — Money is the score. Meaning is the game.

## 8. Reinvestment Rules

### When to Reinvest

| Condition | Reinvestment Allowed | Amount |
|-----------|---------------------|--------|
| GREEN + GREEN | Yes | Up to 50% of surplus |
| GREEN + YELLOW | Conditional | Up to 30% of surplus |
| GREEN + ORANGE | Minimal | Up to 10% of surplus |
| Any ORANGE | Exceptional only | A0 approval required |
| Any RED | No | Zero. Protect runway. |

### When to Protect Runway

**Rule J08-001 — The 6-Month Floor**:
When runway falls below 6 months, no surplus distributes. All excess cash goes to runway extension.

**Rule J08-002 — The Coverage Ratio Ceiling**:
Do not reinvest if reinvestment would reduce coverage ratio below current threshold tier.

**Rule J08-003 — The Asymmetric Bet Rule**:
```
Acceptable bet: Upside > 3× downside AND downside does not breach RED tier
Unacceptable bet: Any bet that could put runway into RED
```
Calculate both scenarios. Document them. If downside breach possible, reject.

**Rule J08-004 — The Wait 72 Hours Rule**:
Any reinvestment decision above $X (set by A0 annually) must wait 72 hours before execution. This prevents urgency-driven misallocation.
