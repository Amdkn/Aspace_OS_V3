# B2-D03 — Risk Management
## Uncertainty Buffers + Insurance Coverage Review

---

## Domain Identity

Risk Management is the **uncertainty layer** — identifying, measuring, and buffering against risks that could destabilize the runway. This includes income volatility, unexpected expenses, market downturns, and liability exposure.

Without risk management, a single adverse event collapses the runway. With it, the system absorbs shocks.

## Core Risk Categories

### 1. Income Volatility Risk
- Revenue concentration in single client/stream
- Seasonal revenue fluctuations
- Payment timing delays

**Mitigation**: Emergency Reserve (D01), revenue diversification

### 2. Market/Investment Risk
- Equity market downturns affecting TIER 2
- Liquidity risk on illiquid investments
- Sequence-of-returns risk

**Mitigation**: Diversified index fund approach, 4% withdrawal rule, cash buffer

### 3. Liability Risk
- Business liability claims
- Personal liability exposure
- Contractual obligations

**Mitigation**: Insurance (D07), entity structures (D06), legal review

### 4. Family Load Risk
- Unexpected family expenses
- Healthcare events
- Education costs

**Mitigation**: Family Stability account (D01), PROTECT account (D07)

## The Asymmetric Bet Rule

```
Acceptable bet: Upside > 3× downside AND downside does not breach RED tier
Unacceptable bet: Any bet that could put runway into RED
```

Calculate both scenarios. Document them. If downside breach possible, reject.
