## The 8 John Jones Sales Principles (v1)

### Cluster A — The Offer (the lever before the pitch) — Black Bolt / Iron Man

**S1 — Build a Grand Slam Offer.** Make the offer so complete, targeted and reassuring that refusing
it feels like a logical *loss* ("feel stupid for not buying"). The offer, not the pitch, is the
primary lever. (Hormozi, $100M Offers.)

**S2 — The Value Equation governs every offer decision.**
`Valeur = (Résultat rêvé × Probabilité perçue de réussite) / (Délai de réalisation × Effort & sacrifice)`.
Push the numerator toward infinity (bigger dream, more credible proof), drive the denominator toward
zero (faster, easier). Every offer tweak answers: *which term am I moving?* (Hormozi.)

**S3 — Risk reversal removes the friction.** Strong guarantees that absorb the buyer's perceived risk
are what unlock the close. Inversion of risk is a core component of the irresistible offer, not a
discount. (Hormozi — Doctor Strange/Professor X read the hidden objection it neutralizes.)

### Cluster B — Pricing (value, never cost) — Namor / Martian Manhunter

**S4 — Premium pricing, decoupled from cost.** Set high prices disconnected from direct delivery cost.
A high price *raises* perceived value, attracts better-fit clients, and funds superior service +
guarantee. We never enter a price war or commoditize. (Hormozi.) *Discount >15% requires Wonder Woman
(Finance) sign-off — Illuminati anti-pattern.*

### Cluster C — The Model (decouple revenue from time) — Martian Manhunter

**S5 — Decouple revenue from hours worked.** Escape the freelancer ceiling where income = time billed.
Sell value/outcomes, not hours. The whole point is asymmetry: revenue that scales without linear time.
(Stop Freelancing.)

**S6 — Freelancer → premium consulting firm (solutions architect).** Shift from the interchangeable
prestataire-à-l'heure to the positioned cabinet billing strategic, value-based solutions. Sales sells
the diagnosis + architecture; Ops encapsulates the delivery (bridge to Ops P19/P21, and Growth's
$100M-agency mid-market positioning).

### Cluster D — Acquisition & Proof (scale on evidence) — Mr Fantastic / Doctor Strange

**S7 — A predictable acquisition funnel, platform-independent.** Build a repeatable, predictable
acquisition system independent of intermediation platforms (Upwork/Fiverr). Conversion scales by
*system*, not by hustle. (Stop Freelancing.)

**S8 — ROI case studies are the closing proof.** Leverage past results: package proven successes
(quantified ROI case studies, reusable SOPs/templates) as undeniable social proof that justifies
premium prices to decision-makers. Evidence closes; cold volume exhausts. (Stop Freelancing.)

### Cluster E — Discovery & Qualification (v2) — Mr Fantastic / Doctor Strange

**S9 — Pre-sell: the ASK before the build.** Validate demand with a *paying commitment* before the
product exists — the first sale precedes the product. Asking for the sale is a trainable skill; "no"
costs nothing. (Million Dollar Weekend, Kagan.) *Facet of Sales only — bootstrap/validation live in
`AREA_STANDARD` P4 + Growth; we point, not duplicate (ADR-MESH-L2-001).*

**S10 — Problem-centric discovery (diagnose before you pitch).** Run S.P.I.N. questioning
(Situation → Problem → Implication → Need-payoff) and sell the **GAP** between the quantified current
state and future state — the size of the gap IS the size of the deal. The rep is a physician first;
no quantified gap, no deal. (SPIN Selling + Gap Selling.) (Mr Fantastic — discovery deep-dive.)

**S11 — Qualification rigor & multi-threading (MEDDIC).** Map Metrics, Economic buyer, Decision
criteria/process, Identify pain, Champion. Never run single-threaded; no reachable economic buyer =
decline (the R2 gate). (Doctor Strange — probabilistic qualification; squad canon MEDDIC.)

### Cluster F — Persuasion, Objection & Negotiation (v2) — Iron Man / Professor X / Namor / Black Bolt

**S12 — Teach, Tailor, Take control (the Challenger).** Bring a commercial **insight** that reframes
how the buyer sees their own business; tailor per stakeholder; assert control of the process (price,
timeline, next step) without aggression. The Challenger outsells the relationship-builder. (Dixon &
Adamson.) (Iron Man — insight via demo/ROI; Black Bolt — control of the close.)

**S13 — Tactical empathy & objection handling.** Labeling ("it sounds like…"), mirroring, and
calibrated questions ("how am I supposed to do that?") make the counterpart solve the problem; "no"
is safe; aim for "that's right". **Never split the difference** — the 50/50 compromise is often the
worst deal. (Voss.) (Professor X reads the hidden objection; Namor negotiates.)

**S14 — Speed-to-close discipline.** A qualified deal that sits loses; drive momentum, always book the
next step, compress the cycle. (Dan Martell speed theme; squad gate: velocity <21d SQL→signed.)
(Black Bolt — closer.)

### Cluster G — Field Sales craft (v3) — Dan Martell, transcript-grounded — Black Bolt / Namor / Mr Fantastic

> Distilled from the **now-real** Dan Martell `06_Sales` corpus (27 transcript-grounded fiches), quality-
> gated by Claude Code (0 empty-transcript / 0 boilerplate / 0 spam). The core Sales DNA lives in
> "27 Years of No Bullsh*t Sales Advice" (`KEXTgvbqx6s`) + "$100M Business that Never Loses Customers"
> (`Tu4ZFSRg8Zo`). ⚠️ Many of the 27 fiches are really mindset/wealth (Buyback→People PE25,
> Buy/Borrow/Die→Finance) — only genuine Sales craft is taken here.

**S15 — The Doctor Frame: diagnose before you prescribe; hold frame control.** Refuse to give price or
solution before a complete diagnosis — position as the clinician who won't prescribe without examining.
The amateur lets the prospect dictate the call ("just give me the price"); the professional controls the
frame. (Sharpens S10 discovery into a *frame-control* rule.) (Mr Fantastic diagnoses; Black Bolt holds frame.)

**S16 — Qualify large→narrow with a Setter/Closer pod; pay on retention, not just signature.** Run a
rigid funnel that eliminates bad-fit prospects fast (profile against your best current clients); a
**Setter** validates real pain + buying intent before the **Closer** spends time. Align comp to
retention/satisfaction, not signature alone — commission-on-signature breeds the oversell that creates
churn. (Sales team leverage; ties Finance pricing gate + Product retention.)

**S17 — Pre-handle objections early, never at the close.** Surface budget / spouse-or-partner /
authority objections *early* in the conversation, where they're addressable — not after the offer, where
they harden into walls. (Complements S11 MEDDIC authority-mapping + S13 Voss.) (Professor X reads them early.)

**S18 — Assume the sale: Takeaway + This-or-That, and lock the next step (BANFAM).** Re-affirm commitment
by pre-confronting start obstacles (the *Takeaway close*); present a binary execution choice with constant
tone (*This or That*, not "are you ready?"); and **never end a meeting without the next step booked in both
calendars** (*Book A Meeting From A Meeting*). Emailing a proposal and hoping is how deals die. (Sharpens
S14 speed-to-close.) (Black Bolt — closer.)

**S19 — Capture referrals at the Aha Moment.** Ask for referrals at the **value milestone** (the client's
first concrete win), not randomly and not at signature — ~30% of organic growth is left on the table by
asking at the wrong time or not at all. The sale extends past the transaction into systematic referral. (Namor/Mr Fantastic.)

**S20 — Sell retention; never discount to keep a client.** The **Growth Ceiling** is real — churn
mathematically caps growth, so plugging the leak beats pure acquisition. Sales **owns** the value-framing
that retains: *Speed-to-First-Value* (Click-Click-Value), the targeted value-event, expand/share-of-wallet
— and the iron rule **never cut price to retain** (it betrays full-payers and erodes perceived value →
Finance pricing gate, S4). ⚠️ The retention *mechanics* (onboarding golden-path, clickstream, cancellation-
capture system, dummy-proof UX) are **Product-owned** (Flash) — Sales points, never duplicates (ADR-MESH-L2-001).

**S21 — Segment by intent-depth, not B2B/B2C; price by target + discourse, build a value ladder.** "B2B vs
B2C" is the wrong axis — segment by **buyer-intent depth** (5 tiers: B2B indépendant · B2B PME/groupe · B2C
casual · B2C prosumer/hobby-objective · B2C usage-pro/investor). The **same engine sells ~8× higher** to a PME
than to an indépendant — driven by the *offer + discourse*, not features (the Tizi 49€/mo → Teachizi Plus
4000€/an proof). So never blanket-discount a premium offer for the same segment (money left on the table);
instead **add a lower tier for a different segment** (a *value ladder*). Pricing framing (per-month / per-year
/ at-signup) *designates the segment*, it is not cosmetic. (`06_Sales/resource_10saas_b2b_b2c_5segments.md`;
ties Finance value-ladder pricing + S4.)

> Council, Buy/Borrow/Die, reinvestment) is **not** Sales doctrine — it lives in **People (PE25 Buy-Back)**
> and **Finance (F14/F19)**. Sales took only the conversion + retention-framing craft.

## Sales ↔ Growth ↔ Product ↔ Ops boundary

- **Growth (Superman)** = acquisition (qualified pipeline) → hands an MQL/SQL to Sales.
- **Sales (John Jones)** = **conversion** (offer + value equation + pricing + close → cash).
- **Product (Flash)** = value (activation × retention) → keeps what Sales closed.
- **Ops (Batman)** = repeatability → encapsulates delivery so Sales sells the *result*, not the hours (S5/S6 ↔ Ops P21).
One datum, one owner (ADR-MESH-L2-001) — domains point, never copy.
