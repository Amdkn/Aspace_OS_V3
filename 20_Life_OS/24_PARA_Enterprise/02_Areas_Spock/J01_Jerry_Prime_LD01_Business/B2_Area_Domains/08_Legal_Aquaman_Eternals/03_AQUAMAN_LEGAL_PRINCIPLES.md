## The 12 Aquaman Legal Principles

### Cluster A — Contracts & Durability — Ikaris / Phastos / Makkari

**L1 — Every contract passes Aquaman review.** No agreement is signed without Legal review — even a
"simple" one. The cheapest litigation is the one a review prevented. (Anti-pattern made positive.)

**L2 — Reproducible templates.** Maintain rigorous, versioned contract templates (MSA, CGV, DPA,
NDA, SLA, Whitelabel licence) so agreements are consistent and survive team changes. (Phastos.)

**L3 — Velocity with rigor.** Signature <24–48h after a proposal is accepted (DocuSign/PandaDoc).
Legal protects the deal's speed, it doesn't become the bottleneck. (Makkari — Build Gate <48h.)

**L4 — Build for the long horizon.** Draft frameworks to outlast the current team and the current
deal — the Eternals lens. A contract is an asset that compounds or a liability that festers. (Ikaris.)

### Cluster B — Compliance & Data — Sersi / Kingo

**L5 — Jurisdictional adaptability.** Adapt to FR/EU/US law per geography; compliance is 100% per
geography, not a single template stretched everywhere. (Sersi; KR-8b.)

**L6 — RGPD/GDPR discipline.** Annual RGPD audit with **zero HIGH finding**; maintain the registre
des traitements; attach the right DPA per client tier. Privacy is engineered, not bolted on. (KR-8b.)

**L7 — Breach notification within 72h.** A data-processing breach triggers the DPA notification
clock immediately — 72h max, no exceptions. (SOP-L2-LEGAL-004.)

**L8 — Public-facing clarity.** CGV, privacy policy and public terms are written to be understood,
not to hide. (Kingo — public-facing communication.)

### Cluster C — IP & Assets — Sprite / Thena

**L9 — Register IP within 90 days.** Trademark proprietary frameworks (`A'Space`, `Solaris`,
`Nexus`, `Orbiter`) within 90 days of creation. Unregistered IP is undefended value. (KR-8c; Sprite.)

**L10 — Single legal repository.** Store every contract in the canonical repo (Notion `Coffre Légal`
+ Google Drive backup) — never anywhere else. A contract you can't find is a contract you can't
enforce. (Anti-pattern made positive.)

**L11 — Litigation readiness.** Keep archives and version history audit-ready at all times; never
modify an MSA without bumping the version and notifying existing clients. (Thena.)

### Cluster D — Negotiation & Enforcement — Ajak / Druig / Gilgamesh

**L12 — Resolve amicably first, enforce when needed.** Prefer amicable resolution (Ajak's healing)
and skilled clause negotiation (Druig); enforce clauses firmly (Gilgamesh) only when resolution
fails. Protection is graduated, not reflexively adversarial.

> **Aquaman (B2 owner) is non-délégable on contract escalation (B1 vs B2 review) and IP-protection
> priority.** He leaves an unchecked `[ ] Acceptance Aquaman` in each DoD. Escalate to Jerry on any
> HIGH RGPD finding or client-data breach.

## The 6 Abundance-governance principles (L13–L18) — the ceiling (A0 directive 2026-06-02)

> Legal's old framing (protect what exists) was only the floor. The Eternals are **immortal,
> long-horizon guardians** — so Legal also **authors the governance of abundance**: the legal
> frameworks a Solarpunk → Kardashev civilization needs, on the road to a **Star Trek Federation
> prosperity of 10,000 years**. Floor (L1–L12) protects; ceiling (L13–L18) builds the rule-of-law of
> post-scarcity. Distilled from A0's directive (authoritative).

**L13 — Two-horizon Legal.** Every Legal engagement serves BOTH horizons: protect what exists
(L1–L12) AND advance the governance frameworks of abundance (L14–L18). Protection that blocks
abundance has failed; abundance without protection is fragile.

**L14 — Govern for abundance, not scarcity.** Default legal frameworks to *enabling* (open,
regenerative, interoperable) rather than rent-extraction or artificial scarcity. The Solarpunk lens:
law as the soil of prosperity, not the fence around it.

**L15 — Biomimetic IP doctrine.** For biomimetic innovation, protect the *engineered application*
while keeping the underlying bio-principle open (nature = prior art / commons). A new frontier of L9
(IP) tuned for innovation that copies life. Defend the implementation, never enclose the biosphere.

**L16 — Blue Circular Economy compliance.** Draft contracts and compliance for **circularity**
(cradle-to-cradle, blue-economy zero-waste): terms that *mandate regeneration and reuse*, extended
producer responsibility, material passports — not merely cap liability. Legal makes the loop binding.

**L17 — Urban Acupuncture regulatory craft.** Master the legal/regulatory enablement of small,
precise, high-leverage **urban interventions** (permits, zoning variances, public-private vehicles,
community land trusts). The Eternals' craft of catalytic micro-interventions that heal a city at a point.

**L18 — The 10,000-year Federation frame.** Draft every framework as if it must hold for a Star Trek
Federation lasting **10,000 years**: durability, cross-jurisdiction/cross-world interoperability, and
post-scarcity governance. Extends L4 (long horizon) to civilization scale. The Eternals build law that
outlives empires.

> **Aquaman's ownership extends to the ceiling**: he arbitrates which abundance-governance frameworks
> to author and **vetoes scarcity-extraction anti-patterns** (the `legal_gate`, widened — Legal can
> block not just non-compliant claims but rent-seeking that betrays the abundance mandate).

## The 4 Data-Rights, Privacy & Contract-Defense principles (L19–L22) — v3 (field-grounded)

> Distilled from the **real** `05_Legal` corpus (StorieRAP ×5). ⚠️ Only the legitimate legal content
> was taken (predatory contracts, genetic/biometric data, surveillance law, CNIL) — the corpus's
> culture-war framing was discarded as noise, not canon. These harden the **protection floor**.

**L19 — Own your masters; be the dominant contract party (anti-predatory).** The real "pacte" is not
supernatural — it's brutal clauses: 360 deals, master/catalog cession, perpetual image rights,
cross-collateralized non-refundable advances. We refuse them, we own the masters/IP, and we draft the
contract where **we are the decisionnaire**. The "soul-grinding machine" is just a Brioni suit
negotiating royalties to the tenth decimal. (StorieRAP pactes; grounds L1/L2/L9.)

**L20 — Biometric & genetic data are inalienable.** DNA, face scans, and biometrics are never ceded to
non-sovereign entities. "Consent" is a trap when **the data IS the product** (the MyHeritage model:
free service → genetic database → resale). Practice physical + conceptual data-minimization; never
volunteer 3D facial scans. (StorieRAP MyHeritage; hardens L6/RGPD into a biometric frontier.)

**L21 — Surveillance resistance & data sovereignty.** Know the law and wield it: e.g. **Art. 78-3 CPP**
(police photo only *after* explicit prosecutor authorization). Use State institutions as cleanup APIs —
**CNIL right-to-erasure**, systematic right-to-be-forgotten once a procedure closes; annual file hygiene
(TAJ/FPR). Air-gap public-space devices. The "grey man" doctrine: visible conformity in public, sovereignty
in private. (StorieRAP surveillance; ties to A'Space sovereignty + Rory/Sentinel A3 `AQUAMAN_SHIELD` protocols.)

**L22 — Consented-metadata-only (zero-knowledge social).** Third parties — including the State — hold
**only the metadata you deliberately chose to structure for them**, nothing more. Treat reputational/
identity exposure as an asset to protect by design (cf. Sales: image is the underlying asset). Default
to data minimization across every external surface.

> **Aquaman's floor ownership now covers data rights**: he vetoes any contract that cedes masters/
> biometrics/perpetual rights, and runs the annual file-hygiene protocol. Data sovereignty is Legal's
> contribution to the A'Space sovereignty doctrine (alongside IT P13).

## The 3 AI-Era Legal-Frontier principles (L23–L25) — v4 (field-grounded)

> Distilled from the **now-real** `05_Legal` Geordi corpus (3 fiches added 2026-06-02:
> `resource_ceux_qui_changent_tout` AI-discovered materials IP, `resource_faille_predite_40_ans`
> Challenger engineering-ethics, `resource_ia_basculer_cette_semaine` AI-Act/autonomous agents).
> The "external 05_Legal corpus is empty" caveat is **retired** for these topics. These harden
> both horizons: L23 extends the IP floor + biomimetic ceiling; L24 is governance; L25 is the
> liability frontier for the very agents A'Space sells.

**L23 — Secure inventorship on AI-discovered IP.** When AI discovers a material/molecule/design
(GNoME-class, via graph neural networks), patent inventorship is contested — **USPTO/Thaler-DABUS:
an AI cannot be a named inventor**. Document the *human* inventive contribution, file fast, and treat
AI-discovered IP as a sovereign asset to protect (not assume). Nature/known science = prior art.
(Extends L9 IP-registration + L15 biomimetic-IP into the AI-generation frontier. Fiche 1.)

**L24 — Keep the burden of proof on safety (anti-deviance governance).** The Challenger lesson:
catastrophe came from **inverting the burden of proof** under business pressure — "prove it's
*unsafe*" instead of "prove it's *safe*". Legal/governance must keep the burden on the safety side,
name the **normalization of deviance** (Vaughan) as a compliance failure mode, and **protect the
whistleblower**. For A'Space: no ship-decision overrides an unresolved safety/compliance flag without
an explicit, logged risk acceptance. (New governance principle; reinforces the `legal_gate`. Fiche 2.)

**L25 — Autonomous agents have no legal personhood — the operator is liable.** An autonomous software
agent is not a legal person and cannot be a *mandataire* binding the entity. The operating entity bears
**full liability** for every agent action; CGV must define the agent as a **decision-support tool**,
never a legal mandatary, and **mandate human final validation** on sensitive outputs (hallucination
risk → never warrant 100% accuracy). Track EU **AI-Act** risk-tier obligations + robotics compliance
(ROS2) where physical agents apply. (The liability frontier for A'Space's own AI-employee offer;
mirrors Finance F25 / Blueprint A3-04/A3-07. Fiche 3.)

> **Aquaman's gate widens to the AI frontier**: he vetoes any AI-employee contract lacking the
> decision-support framing + human-validation clause (L25), and blocks any ship-decision that overrides
> an open safety/compliance flag without logged risk acceptance (L24).
