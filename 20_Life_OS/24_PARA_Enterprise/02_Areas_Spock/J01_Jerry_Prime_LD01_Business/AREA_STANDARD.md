## Operating Principles

### P1 — Work ON the Business, Not IN the Business
**Source**: *The E-Myth Revisited* (Gerber)

> Jerry Prime is the Manager who designs the system. The Technicians run it.

- **Rule**: Jerry spends max 20% of week on delivery/tactical. Minimum 50% on strategic design.
- **Escalation**: If tactical exceeds 20% for 2 consecutive weeks → declare emergency
- **Anti-pattern**: Jerry doing anything a B3 squad can do. If a B3 can do it, it IS a B3 task.

### P2 — Build Businesses, Not Jobs
**Source**: *Built to Sell* (Warrilow)

> A business that depends on you is not a business — it is a job.

- **Rule**: Every offer must be sellable without Jerry present
- **Test**: Can this offer run for 90 days with zero Jerry input? If no → redesign or sunset
- **Trigger**: Any offer requiring Jerry's personal execution to deliver → reclassified as "Job", not Business
- **Target**: Minimum 3 offers with zeroJerry dependency within 12 months

### P3 — Delegate WHO, Not HOW
**Source**: *Who Not How* (Sullivan)

> The question is always: WHO can do this? Never: HOW can I do this?

- **Rule**: Every task that recurs 3+ times becomes a WHO delegation, not a HOW process
- **Decision Matrix**:
  - Recurring task → Find WHO (delegate to existing B3 or hire/contract)
  - One-time novel task → Jerry may solve HOW directly, then systematize
  - Never build a HOW process for recurring work without first attempting WHO
- **Anti-pattern**: Writing SOPs before finding the WHO. SOPs without WHO = job, not business.

### P4 — Bootstrap Before You Scale
**Source**: *The Million Dollar Week-End* (Huber)

> Revenue before overhead. Proof before investment.

- **Rule**: No headcount before $10K/month per person on net revenue
- **Runway test**: Can new hire cost be covered by net new revenue they generate? Net only — not gross.
- **Phase gates**:
  - $0–$10K MRR: Solo + contractors only
  - $10K–$50K MRR: 1–3 B3 squad members
  - $50K–$200K MRR: B2 managers per domain
  - $200K+: B2 + B3 fractional + specialized contractors
- **Bootstrapping override**: If opportunity cost > $50K from delay, exception allowed with B1 approval

### P5 — Offer Architecture Before Offer Execution
**Source**: *$100M Offers* (Hormozi)

> The offer is the business. Bad offer = bad business. Good offer = good business. Great offer = great business.

- **Offer Audit**: Every quarter, audit all active offers against the $100M Offer rubric:
  1. **Dream Outcome**: Does it promise a dramatic, measurable transformation?
  2. **Perceived Likelihood**: High probability of success in customer's mind?
  3. **Three Times**: Price is ≤ 1/3 of delivered value?
  4. **独特 Selling Proposition**: Why THIS offer vs alternatives?
  5. **Easy & Risk-Free**: Is the commitment to buy low-friction?
- **Offer refresh**: Any offer scoring < 3/5 on any dimension → redesign within 60 days
- **Price anchor**: Default to value-based pricing. Time-and-materials only when scope is genuinely unknowable.

### P6 — Brand Equity Compounds, It Does Not Fluctuate
**Source**: *Billion Dollar Brand Club* (Demto)

> Brand is the only asset that appreciates while you sleep.

- **Rule**: Brand investment = 10% of gross revenue minimum, non-negotiable
- **Allocation priority**: Brand > Demand Gen > Lead Gen (brand-less)
- **No brand debt**: Any offer or client that damages brand equity must be exited. Revenue that damages brand is not revenue — it is liability.
- **Brand audit**: Semi-annual review of brand perception across Nexus/Solaris/Orbiter segments
- **Crisis threshold**: Any press coverage or social sentiment shift > 15% negative → immediate B1 escalation

### P7 — Cerritos First, Jerry Second
**Source**: *The E-Myth Revisited* + LD01 routing doctrine

> Ideas do not live in Jerry's head. They live in Cerritos until Picard moves them to Summer's Verse.

- **Rule**: Every idea, opportunity, or strategic input routes through Cerritos first
- **Jerry's filter**: Jerry's role is to set criteria, not to receive directly
- **Routing criteria** (see Cerritos Handoff Protocol below)
- **No Jerry-first execution**: If Jerry is the first to act on an opportunity, the routing has failed

### P8 — Quarterly Rocks Over Quarterly Reports
**Source**: E-Myth + LD01 B1/B2/B3 structure

> Measurement without action is procrastination. Action without measurement is gambling.

- **Rule**: 4 Rocks per quarter, maximum. If you have more than 4, you have none.
- **Rock criteria**: Each Rock must have a start state, end state, and 90-day deadline
- **Weekly scorecard**: 5 KRs per Rock, updated every Monday
- **Escalation**: Any Rock > 2 weeks behind → emergency session with B1 within 48 hours

## Delegation Framework

### WHO NOT HOW — The Decision Tree

```
TASK RECEIVED
     │
     ▼
Is this recurring? ──No──► One-time / novel ──► Jerry solves HOW once
     │                                        Then systematize → WHO owns it
     │
    Yes
     │
     ▼
Has a WHO been assigned? ──No──► Find WHO before building HOW
     │
     │
    Yes
     │
     ▼
Does WHO have clear outcome + authority + deadline?
     │                    │
    Yes                   No
     │                    │
     ▼                    ▼
Delegate and        Redefine mandate:
trust              Outcome + authority + deadline
     │
     ▼
Does WHO complete? ──No──► Calibrate WHO, not HOW
     │
    Yes
     │
     ▼
Does outcome meet criteria? ──No──► Realign criteria with WHO
     │
    Yes
     │
     ▼
Systemize for next occurrence → Recurring = WHO handles
```

### B3 Marvel Squad Delegation Standards

Each B3 squad member receives:

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| **Outcome** | What "done" looks like — specific, measurable, time-bound |
| **Authority** | What they can decide without escalation |
| **Deadline** | Start date + end date, not just "ASAP" |
| **Resources** | Budget, tools, access — everything needed to succeed |
| **Escalation path** | Who to contact if blocked + response time SLA |

**Jerry's delegation check** (weekly):
- Did the WHO complete? → If no: Was the failure a WHO issue (wrong person) or a HOW issue (bad system)?
- WHO issues → find new WHO. Never persist with wrong WHO.
- HOW issues → redesign the system, not the person.

## Offer Architecture

### The $100M Offer Rubric (Applied)

Every LD01 offer is scored 1–5 on each dimension:

| Dimension | Question | Weight |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| **Dream Outcome** | Does it promise a 3-10x transformation? | 25% |
| **Perceived Likelihood** | Does the customer believe they can achieve it? | 20% |
| **Price vs Value** | Is price ≤ 1/3 of stated value? | 20% |
| **USP** | Why this, not the competitor? | 20% |
| **Low Friction** | Is the next step obvious and risk-free? | 15% |

**Minimum threshold**: 4.0/5.0 weighted average to launch. Below = redesign.

### Offer Lifecycle (Built to Sell)

```
IDEATION → SCOPING → OFFER DESIGN → VALIDATION → LAUNCH → ITERATION → SUNSET
    │           │            │             │           │          │          │
  Jerry        B2           B2           Cerritos   Picard/    Quarterly  B2/Jerry
  filters     designs      applies       tests w/    GTD       review     sunset
                          $100M         real buyers
                          rubric
```

**Validation gate**: Before launch, minimum 3 paying customers OR $10K revenue (whichever first) confirms offer-market fit.

### Offer Portfolio Rules

| Revenue Level | Active Offer Count | Offer Type Mix |
|--------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| < $50K MRR | 1–2 flagship | 1 core, 1 expansion |
| $50K–$200K | 3–4 | 1 flagship, 1-2 volume, 1 premium |
| $200K–$500K | 5–7 | 2 flagship, 2 volume, 1 premium, 1-2 experimental |
| > $500K | 8–12 | Portfolio balanced across Solaris/Nexus/Orbiter |

### Cash Mode Thresholds

| Mode | Trigger (Monthly Revenue) | Jerry's Role | Investment Priority |

| **Orbiter** | > $500K | Architect + Executive | Field network, franchise model |

### Reinvestment Rules

**Surplus allocation** (profit above 12-month runway):

```
Surplus = Actual Cash - (Monthly Burn × 12)

0–25% of surplus → Thunderbolts reserves (cash buffer)
25–50% of surplus → Growth/Guardians (brand + demand gen)
50–75% of surplus → Product/Avengers (offer development)
75–100% of surplus → Ops/Batman (systems + Cerritos infrastructure)
```

**Exception**: Jerry can reallocate up to 25% of any category with B1 approval for strategic opportunities.

### Orbiter-Specific Rules

### Cerritos Minimum Standards

- **Inbox zero**: All inputs acknowledged within 4 hours during business days
- **Routing SLA**: Every idea routed to Picard within 48 hours
- **Escalation**: If Picard has not actioned within 72 hours → Jerry escalates to B1
- **Weekly review**: Jerry + Cerritos operator weekly sync to review routing accuracy

### Picard Summer's Verse Handoff

When Cerritos routes to Picard:

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| **Context** | Full background on why this idea matters |
| **Desired outcome** | What success looks like |
| **Constraints** | What's fixed vs flexible |
| **Deadline** | When decision or action is needed |
| **Authority** | What Picard can decide independently |
| **Escalation path** | When to push back vs when to act |
