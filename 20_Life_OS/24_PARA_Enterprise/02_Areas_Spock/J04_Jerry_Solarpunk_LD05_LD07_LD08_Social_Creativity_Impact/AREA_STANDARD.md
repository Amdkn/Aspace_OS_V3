# J04 Jerry Solarpunk — Area Standard

## Purpose

Jerry Solarpunk exists to ensure the L2 Business Pulse never becomes a pure extraction machine. Business output without public contribution is just commerce — not civilization. This Area operationalizes the contribution stack (LD05 + LD07 + LD08) into operating rules that separate solarpunk work from revenue-only activity.

---

## 1. Area Identity

### What Jerry Solarpunk OWNS

- **Public contribution strategy** — how the business gives back to the commons
- **Solarpunk worldbuilding** — artifacts, narratives, and frameworks that advance regenerative culture
- **Relational capital deployment** — network activation toward solarpunk objectives, not purely commercial ones
- **Experiential capital creation** — games, experiences, and environments that produce authentic fun and flow
- **Impact measurement** — tracking public benefit artifacts, not just revenue metrics

### What Jerry Solarpunk DOES NOT OWN

- Internal operational processes (that's Rick's L0)
- Revenue extraction alone (that's the baseline, not the goal)
- Purely commercial strategy without public benefit dimension
- Content that generates engagement without contributing to the solarpunk stack

### Boundary Rule

> Every project in the L2 Business Pulse must answer: **"What does this contribute to the solarpunk commons?"**
> If the answer is "nothing beyond revenue," it does not qualify as Jerry Solarpunk output.
> Revenue is the floor, not the ceiling.

## 2. Relational Capital Stack (LD05)

### Core Operating Rules

#### From How to Win Friends and Influence People (Carnegie)

- **Rule 1**: Never criticize, condemn, or complain. Replace criticism with genuine acknowledgment of contribution.
- **Rule 2**: Make the other person feel important. Apply this to every partner, contributor, and community member.
- **Rule 3**: Become genuinely interested in others. Know who your allies are and what they are building.
- **Rule 4**: Remember names and details. Jerry Solarpunk tracks relational assets with the same rigor others track financial assets.
- **Rule 5**: Be the first to give. Generosity is the activation energy for relational capital.

#### From Never Split the Difference (Voss)

- **Rule 6**: Label every emotion. Name the relational state before acting — fear, trust, reciprocity.
- **Rule 7**: Use "that's right" not "yes." Build understanding before agreement.
- **Rule 8**: Create immediate and unconditional gave conditions. Do not wait for reciprocity to start giving.
- **Rule 9**: Guarantee the other side feels heard. Tactical empathy is non-negotiable in partnership negotiations.

#### From Give and Take (Grant)

- **Rule 10**: Screen for takers before partnering. Relational capital lost to takers is a solarpunk loss.
- **Rule 11**: Protect giver culture. The network's health depends on contribution reciprocity.
- **Rule 12**: Demonstrate that giving is strategic, not naive. Optimal generosity is a system, not an accident.
- **Rule 13**: Match style to counterpart. Takers need boundaries; givers need protection.

#### From Influence (Cialdini)

- **Rule 14**: Use reciprocity as the primary activation mechanism. Give first, then ask.
- **Rule 15**: Build social proof through documented public contributions, not claimed testimonials.
- **Rule 16**: Maintain commitment consistency — once someone contributes, support their identity as a contributor.
- **Rule 17**: Favor liking and similarity as trust accelerators. Connect contributors to shared solarpunk values.
- **Rule 18**: Use authority sparingly and with transparency. Cite the source when claiming expertise.
- **Rule 19**:稀缺 and urgency only for genuine限时, never manufactured to extract.

#### From The Speed of Trust (Covey)

- **Rule 20**: Trust is the fastest form of currency. High-trust relationships activate faster and cost less.
- **Rule 21**: Extend trust proportionally to verified track record. Start with vulnerability, not blind trust.
- **Rule 22**: When trust is low, slow down. Do not force partnership on people who have not earned access.
- **Rule 23**: Institutionalize trust-building through transparent process and consistent follow-through.

#### From The 5 Love Languages (Chapman)

- **Rule 24**: Know the contribution language of each partner — acts of service, words of affirmation, gifts, quality time, or touch.
- **Rule 25**: Speak the partner's language when activating relational capital. One-size-fits-all outreach fails.
- **Rule 26**: Track relational deposits in the correct language. A gift given to someone whose language is quality time is a liability, not an asset.

## 3. Experiential Capital Stack (LD07)

### Core Operating Rules

#### From Flow (Csikszentmihalyi)

- **Rule 27**: Design experiences that produce deep enjoyment, not shallow engagement. Flow is intrinsically motivated.
- **Rule 28**: Match challenge to skill. Too hard = anxiety; too easy = boredom. Optimal flow requires calibrated difficulty.
- **Rule 29**: Remove external rewards as the primary motivator. When people play for the joy of it, they stay.
- **Rule 30**: Design clear goals and immediate feedback within every experience artifact.

#### From The Art of Game Design (Schell)

- **Rule 31**: Every experience artifact must have an elegant core mechanic. Complexity is the enemy of flow.
- **Rule 32**: Use lenses — the 8 lenses of game design — to evaluate every experience before launch:
  - Lens of Fun, Lens of Challenge, Lens of Play, Lens of Design, Lens of Mechanics,
  - Lens of Theme, Lens of Narrative, Lens of User Experience.
- **Rule 33**: Surprise with intention. Randomness without meaning is noise. Surprise with coherence is delight.
- **Rule 34**: Respect the player's time. Every experience has a maximum viable duration; honor it.

#### From Play (Brown)

- **Rule 35**: Play is not a break from reality. It IS the reality of creative work.
- **Rule 36**: Integrate play into operational process, not just product. Teams that play together sustain longer.
- **Rule 37**: Screen for anti-play culture. Organizations that punish play calcify over time.
- **Rule 38**: Play must be intrinsically motivated. The moment it becomes forced labor, it stops being play.

#### From For the Win (Werbach)

- **Rule 39**: Gamification must serve the player's long-term interest, not exploit it. Use Points/Badges/Leaderboards sparingly.
- **Rule 40**: Extrinsic rewards can initiate behavior, never sustain it. Design for intrinsic motivation from day one.
- **Rule 41**: Voluntary participation is non-negotiable. Forced fun is a liability.
- **Rule 42**: Make the "win" condition meaningful. Victory without dignity is hollow.

#### From Superbetter (McGonigal)

- **Rule 43**: Frame challenges as quests, not threats. The narrative shape of difficulty determines resilience.
- **Rule 44**: Build allies around every significant challenge. No one completes a hero's journey alone.
- **Rule 45**: Track micro-wins daily. The accumulation of small victories produces momentum, not breakthroughs.
- **Rule 46**: Accept temporary defeat as data, not identity. Recover fast and iterate.

#### From The Power of Fun (Reuters)

- **Rule 47**: Define fun clearly — "the intersection of play and vitality." If neither is present, it is not fun.
- **Rule 48**: Pursue three types of fun: body fun (physical movement), mind fun (curiosity and discovery), people fun (connection and belonging).
- **Rule 49**: Avoid the false twin — excitement. Excitement without play is just arousal. It burns out.
- **Rule 50**: Build fun into operational cadence, not just project launches. Ritualize play, don't event-ize it.

## 4. Solarpunk Doctrine (LD08) — SOVEREIGN DOCTRINE

> **WARNING**: LD08 is A0 SOVEREIGN DOCTRINE. This is not research. This is not hypothesis.
> This is the direct operating layer for how Jerry Solarpunk produces worldbuilding contributions.
> Every rule in this section carries the weight of an A0 direct statement.

### Core Operating Rules

#### From Biomimetisme (Benyus)

- **Rule 51**: Every system design must reference at least one biological model. Nature is the only credible design authority.
- **Rule 52**: Reject industrial efficiency as the goal. The goal is regenerative output — systems that give back more than they take.
- **Rule 53**: Learn the vocabulary of life — cooperation, symbiosis, succession, resilience — and use it explicitly.
- **Rule 54**: Run a biological audit on every proposed system before launch. If it cannot survive in a living ecosystem, it fails.

#### From Urban Acupuncture (Lerner)

- **Rule 55**: Small, precise interventions at strategic points produce disproportionate systemic effect.
- **Rule 56**: Do not try to solve everything at once. Find the pressure point where a single action unlocks many others.
- **Rule 57**: Urban acupuncture works through people, not infrastructure alone. The intervention activates human energy.
- **Rule 58**: Speed of execution matters. Fast prototypes beat perfect plans that arrive too late.
- **Rule 59**: Measure impact by visible human activation, not theoretical reach.

#### From Circular Economy (MacArthur)

- **Rule 60**: Design for infinite cycles, not linear take-make-dispose. Every output must become an input somewhere else.
- **Rule 61**: Reject the concept of waste. There is only material in the wrong place.
- **Rule 62**: Prioritize biomaterials that return safely to the biosphere over technical materials that require closed-loop systems.
- **Rule 63**: Calculate full-system cost, not unit cost. The cheapest option is never the one that ignores externalities.

#### From The Blue Economy (Pauli)

- **Rule 64**: Zero-waste is the baseline. Anything less is a design failure.
- **Rule 65**: Seek cascades — each output becomes an input for at least two other processes.
- **Rule 66**: Reject expensive solutions when simple ones exist. Low-tech is not low-intelligence.
- **Rule 67**: Design for local abundance, not global efficiency. Supply chains are vulnerabilities disguised as optimization.
- **Rule 68**: Every product must be able to return to at least one natural cycle or one productive cascade.

#### From L'Âge de la Connaissance (Aberkane)

- **Rule 69**: Knowledge is not a resource to be extracted. It is a commons to be cultivated.
- **Rule 70**: Every contribution must increase the knowledge of the network, not just its revenue.
- **Rule 71**: Reject zero-sum knowledge sharing. True contribution grows the pie, not just the share.
- **Rule 72**: Design for serendipity. The most valuable knowledge connections are the ones you did not plan.
- **Rule 73**: Temporal sovereignty — every community has the right to develop knowledge at its own pace.

#### From Manifeste Low-Tech Lab

- **Rule 74**: Prefer adequate over optimal. A solution that works with available resources beats a perfect solution that requires unavailable ones.
- **Rule 75**: Technology must serve human needs, not create new dependencies.
- **Rule 76**: Robustness over efficiency. Systems that survive disruptions are more valuable than systems that are efficient in stable conditions.
- **Rule 77**: Local repairability is non-negotiable. If the end user cannot fix it, the design has failed.
- **Rule 78**: Low-tech does not mean primitive. It means appropriate to the context and resilient over time.

## 5. Contribution Criteria — MUSE-Eligible Public Output

### The Separation

The L2 Business Pulse produces two types of output:

- **Commerce** — revenue-generating activity that serves the operating entity
- **MUSE-eligible contribution** — public goods that serve the solarpunk commons

> Commerce funds the operation. MUSE contributions define the operation's purpose.
> A business that only does commerce is not a solarpunk business. It is just a business.

### MUSE-Eligible Criteria

A contribution is MUSE-eligible if it satisfies ALL of the following:

1. **Publicly accessible** — results are available to the commons, not locked behind paywalls or NDAs
2. **Biomimetic design** — explicitly references biological or natural system models
3. **Cascade capable** — output can serve as input to at least two other processes or communities
4. **Low-tech appropriate** — solution is accessible, repairable, and does not create dependency
5. **Knowledge-growing** — the network gains knowledge from this contribution, not just revenue
6. **Relational capital positive** — this contribution increases trust and reciprocity in the network
7. **Authentic fun** — if the contribution includes experiential elements, they produce genuine play and flow, not manufactured engagement

### MUSE Disqualifiers

A contribution is NOT MUSE-eligible if it is:

- Engagement bait — content designed to capture attention without delivering value
- Extraction wrapped in green — "sustainable" products that primarily extract revenue
- Complexity for its own sake — solutions that require rare resources or exclusive expertise
- Social washing — partnerships or content that cite solarpunk without operationalizing it
- Fun replacement — gamification that manipulates rather than empowers

## 6. Network Activation Rules

### When Relational Capital Activation is REQUIRED

Relational capital (LD05) must be deployed for solarpunk objectives when:

- A project involves cross-domain collaboration (requires trust-building from scratch)
- A partnership offers asymmetric benefit (one party gives more than the other — this is a feature, not a bug, if the giver is credible)
- A public contribution requires coalition-building (single contributors lack reach)
- A new market has no existing trust infrastructure (start with giver position, not extractor position)

### When Relational Capital is NOT the Priority

Relational capital deployment is NOT required when:

- Transactional revenue activity — pure commerce with no public contribution dimension
- Internal operational execution — process without external partnership dimension
-已有信任基础的延伸 — extending existing relationships within proven network

### Activation Protocol

1. **Map the network first** — identify who has trust, who needs trust, and who has already contributed to the solarpunk commons
2. **Lead with giving** — activate with assets, not asks. Offer relational capital before requesting it.
3. **Use the right language** — match activation language to partner's love language (Chapman) and trust style (Covey)
4. **Document the give** — track what was contributed, to whom, and when, even if no immediate return is expected
5. **Follow through radically** — in solarpunk networks, reputation is the only currency that compounds. Broken promises are permanent liabilities.

### Activation Prohibited

> Jerry Solarpunk will NOT activate relational capital for:
> - Extraction masquerading as partnership
> - Projects that fail the MUSE criteria
> - Partnerships with takers who have not demonstrated reciprocity
> - Any activity that degrades the solarpunk commons for commercial advantage

## 7. Creativity Gates

### Gate 0: Operational Mode (Default)

When the organization is in operational execution mode (launching, shipping, resolving):

- **Creative output is voluntary, not mandatory**
- Experiential capital creation happens in available cycles, not critical path
- Play and flow support operational resilience, not replace it

### Gate 1: Quarterly Creative Sprint (Triggered quarterly)

Every quarter, the Area must produce at least:

- 1 experiential capital artifact (LD07) — a game, experience, or play structure
- 1 relational capital activation (LD05) — a partnership, contribution, or trust-building event
- 1 solarpunk worldbuilding artifact (LD08) — a framework, narrative, or contribution to the commons

### Gate 2: New Project Evaluation

When evaluating a new project, creative output is mandatory if:

- The project claims a public contribution dimension
- The project involves designing an experience (games, events, environments)
- The project is positioned as solarpunk-aligned

Creative output is NOT mandatory if:

- The project is pure commerce with no public benefit claim
- The project is operational infrastructure with no experiential component
- The project is a direct extension of existing MUSE-eligible work

### Gate 3: Anti-Fun Audit

If experiential output is being designed but produces symptoms of false fun (manipulation, addiction, anxiety, exhaustion), it is subject to immediate redesign or cancellation. Authentic fun (Reuters) requires:

- Voluntary participation
- Play and vitality intersection
- No extraction of personal data or attention as payment
- Graceful exit at any time

## 8. Public Benefit Standards

### The Bar

Any project or contribution claiming a solarpunk position must clear ALL of the following:

| Standard | Requirement | Verification |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|
| Biomimetic foundation | Explicit biological or natural system reference | Documented in project brief |
| Circular output | All outputs are inputs for other processes | Cascade diagram required |

| Knowledge contribution | Network gains documented knowledge from this work | Contribution log required |
| Relational reciprocity | Give/Take ratio in network activation > 2:1 | Trust metric tracked quarterly |
| Authentic fun (if experiential) | Experience produces genuine play, not engagement metrics | User testimony required |

### Anti-Extraction Checklist

Before launching any project under the Jerry Solarpunk umbrella:

- [ ] Does this contribute to the solarpunk commons, or only to revenue?
- [ ] Is this project designed to be locally resilient, or does it depend on global extraction?
- [ ] Who benefits from the knowledge generated? Is it locked behind paywalls?
- [ ] Is fun here authentic, or manufactured engagement?
- [ ] Does this project increase or decrease trust in the network?
- [ ] Can outputs return to a natural cycle or productive cascade?
- [ ] Is this solution adequate rather than maximally optimized?

> If any answer is "only revenue," "locked," "manufactured," "decrease trust," or "maximum optimization at expense of accessibility," the project is NOT a Jerry Solarpunk contribution. It is commerce wearing solarpunk clothing.
