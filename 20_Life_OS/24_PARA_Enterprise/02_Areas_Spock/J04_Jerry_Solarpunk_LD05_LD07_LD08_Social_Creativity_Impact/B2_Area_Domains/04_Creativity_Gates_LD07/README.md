## 1. Domain Identity

### What This Domain OWNS

Creativity Gates are the flow protection layer — protocols that determine when creative output is mandatory versus when operational execution takes priority. It ensures creativity serves solarpunk goals and guards against forced play.

### What This Domain Does NOT OWN

- Relational asset creation (that's D01)
- Network activation protocols (that's D02)
- Experiential artifact creation (that's D03)
- LD08 doctrine (that's D05-D07)

### Contribution Type

Creativity Gates contribute **flow protection** — the governance rules that determine when to demand creativity versus when to protect operational mode.

---

## 2. The Four Gates

### Gate 0: Operational Mode (Default)

When the organization is in operational execution mode (launching, shipping, resolving):
- **Creative output is voluntary, not mandatory**
- Experiential capital creation happens in available cycles, not critical path
- Play and flow support operational resilience, not replace it

### Gate 1: Quarterly Creative Sprint (Triggered quarterly)

Every quarter, the Area must produce at least:
- 1 experiential capital artifact (LD07) — a game, experience, or play structure
- 1 relational capital activation (LD05) — a partnership, contribution, or trust-building event
- 1 solarpunk worldbuilding artifact (LD08) — a framework, narrative, or contribution to the commons

### Gate 2: New Project Evaluation

Creative output is mandatory if:
- The project claims a public contribution dimension
- The project involves designing an experience (games, events, environments)
- The project is positioned as solarpunk-aligned

Creative output is NOT mandatory if:
- The project is pure commerce with no public benefit claim
- The project is operational infrastructure with no experiential component
- The project is a direct extension of existing MUSE-eligible work

### Gate 3: Anti-Fun Audit

If experiential output produces symptoms of false fun (manipulation, addiction, anxiety, exhaustion), it is subject to immediate redesign or cancellation.

Authentic fun (Reuters) requires:
- Voluntary participation
- Play and vitality intersection
- No extraction of personal data or attention as payment
- Graceful exit at any time

---

## 3. Anti-Fun Audit (Pre-Demand Checklist)

Before any creative output is demanded, run the Anti-Fun Audit:

| Question | If "Performance version of yes" |
|----------|-------------------------------|
| Is creative output genuinely necessary for the work? | Do not demand — operational execution is preferable |
| Does the person have genuine agency in the creative process? | Execute someone else's vision is not creative output |
| Is there time and space for real play? | Deadline-driven production sprint is not creative |
| Is creative work freely chosen? | Social or economic consequence for refusing disqualifies |

**If any answer is "the performance version of yes" — do not demand creative output.**

---

## 4. Flow Priority Matrix

| Context | Priority |
|---------|----------|
| When in flow state | Protect the flow — do not interrupt for administrative or operational matters |
| When in operational mode | Execute — do not demand creative input while in execution mode |
| When in relationship maintenance mode | Connect — do not switch to transactional work |
| When in creative exploration mode | Explore — do not constrain with operational requirements |

---

## 5. LD08 Override Rule

**LD08 (Regenerative Capital) always has priority over LD07 (Experiential Capital) when they conflict.**

Rationale: play that advances extraction is not acceptable. Creativity that destroys regenerative capacity is not acceptable. When in doubt about whether an activity is "true fun" or "false fun," the LD08 test takes precedence.

---

## 6. Creativity During Crisis Protocol

| Crisis Type | Creative Output Rule |
|-------------|---------------------|
| Acute crisis | No creative output required — survive first, create second |
| Chronic crisis | Minimal creative output — maintain minimum viable experiment |
| Recovery phase | Slow return to creative work — do not rush restoration |
| Building phase | Full creative output allowed — this is the time for ambitious work |
| Stable phase | Sustained creative cadence — protect the rhythm |

---

## 7. Engagement Lens (Game Design Applied)

| Game Design Concept | Non-Game Application | Anti-Pattern |
|-------------------|---------------------|--------------|
| **Agency** | Real choice in how to contribute | Fake choices that all lead to same outcome |
| **Mastery** | Getting better at something that matters | Progress theater without skill gain |
| **Belonging** | Being part of something larger | Token inclusion without real voice |
| **Narrative** | Story that makes meaning out of work | Bureaucratic framing that strips meaning |
| **Challenge** | Problems worth solving | Make-work that exists to fill time |
