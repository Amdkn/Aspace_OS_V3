# B2 Domain 05 — Cold Exposure/BLS

## Domain Identity

### What It Protects

Cold Exposure/BLS is the **brown adipose tissue (BAT) and metabolic flexibility domain** — it protects:
- BAT activation (metabolically active fat — burns glucose and fat for heat)
- Metabolic flexibility (ability to switch between fuel sources)
- Cold tolerance resilience (stress adaptation)
- Mood regulation (cold → norepinephrine → dopamine release)
- Immune function (mild cold stress → immune activation)
- HRV resilience via sympathetic adaptation

### Why It Matters

```
Cold exposure → BAT activation → improved glucose metabolism → better cognitive performance
Cold shower (3min) → norepinephrine increase 200–300% → mood improvement
Cold exposure 3×/week → improved insulin sensitivity (metabolic flexibility)
Cold tolerance → reduced inflammatory response to psychological stress
Winter BAT activation → maintained metabolic rate during reduced movement
Cold → brown fat thermogenesis → cognitive blood flow improvement
```

## Jerry's Stewardship Protocol

### Cold Exposure Protocol

```
Morning (after Zone 2 or strength):
  □ Cold shower: 3min at minimum 15°C (60°F)
  □ 30s cold → warm → 30s cold → warm → cold finale pattern
  □ End with cold (forces BAT activation at end)

Weekly additions (if practiced):
  □ Cold plunge or ice bath: 5–10min at 5–10°C (41–50°F)
  □ Post-exercise cold: 2–3min cold shower after Zone 2

Winter protocol (Oct–Mar):
  □ Bathe face in cold water morning and evening (BAT localized to face/neck)
  □ Open window during sleep if room temp >20°C
  □ Outdoor exposure minimum 30min/day regardless of temperature
```

### Monitoring

```
Daily:
  □ Cold shower completed (Y/N)
  □ Duration and temperature (subjective if no thermometer)
  □ Shivering response (GREEN = shivering, ORANGE = mild discomfort, RED = no response)

Weekly:
  □ Sessions completed (minimum 3)
  □ Any days without cold exposure (should be rare, not habitual)
```

## Escalation Rules

```
ORANGE Trigger: Cold exposure <3×/week for 1+ week
  → ORANGE ADVISORY issued
  → Implement minimum cold shower 3×/week (non-negotiable)
  → No excuses for missing: 3min is minimum viable dose
  → If illness: skip, do not compensate

ORANGE Trigger: No shivering response after 3min cold
  → BAT may be deactivated — increase frequency to daily for 2 weeks
  → Check vitamin D levels (winter is most common cause of BAT dysfunction)
  → Consider: longer cold exposure (5min) or colder water

RED Trigger: Any single LD03 RED (especially HRV or Sleep)
  → Cold exposure PROHIBITED during RED recovery
  → Adding cold stress when HRV is RED is counterproductive
  → Resume cold exposure only after HRV returns to GREEN
  → Beth HALT protocol supersedes cold exposure rules
```
